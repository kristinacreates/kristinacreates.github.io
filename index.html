  • Illustrator

Creating the Raptor

Falcons are quick and precise. They soar free before landing on their prey. Strong , agile and light. Seems like a worthy candidate to represent the gaming laptop. Based on my photographs, I took to creating sketches and drawing renditions of my Falcon icon.

It seemed to evolve from ink with many splotches to tightening up the logo.Lessening the details worked! Thus, I simplified the Falcon but captured its curved claw and feathers.

In Conclusion

Designing the Falcon to encompass the "Soar Free" was a great challenge. I realized I focused on the details. When I stepped away and saw all the ink blotted curves, it took away from what the Falcon is. It was interesting how my design became stronger by removing the details so the eye focuses on the simple strong linesit conveys.

Designing a package to both look attractive and to also protect its contents come hand in hand. In the future. I plan to revise and expand on this Raptor laptop brand.

MokProto : Prototyping Mockup Tool for Game Designers

UX Design

11 Weeks

  • Figma
  • Miro
  • Lookback
  • Zeplin

Challenge Overview

I was interested in creating a mobile app for Game Designers to create game prototypes and showcase their game mockup. My platform aimed at providing creativity tools as a digital productivity sandbox for on-the-go Game Designers.

Goal

The goal was to examine how Game Designers use tools to prototype and create mockups of their game ideas. This will also help assess what opportunities or needs would be for a Game Prototype Mockup App. The findings would assist the direction of the Product and its features.

Case Study

During the Mokproto Case Study, I iterated a complete design process which spanned concept, research study, design prototype, and useability testing.

Throughout the project, there were several key findings within user interviews and surveys,

Key Findings

  • 2 out of 4 Participants find the right container for their work rather than their work conforms to its container.

  • Current prototype tools are more centralized on desktop applications than mobile apps.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Using the Figma concepts I learned through Udacity, I kept that in mind while beginning to sketch out my Concepts. This was easier for me to illustrate my ideas before constructing a Lo Fi design.

Login Screen

I designed a basic outline of the login screen and option to create a new account if the person is a new user.

Welcome Screen

After logging in, the user is greeted with the general welcome screen which displays some tutorials and intro information within the circles which can be horizontally scrolled.

Navigation Screen

I wanted to design where the tools would be designated in. I figured it would be easier to place in the bottom of the design so the person could use it one handed.

Quick Draw Screen

After selecting the pencil icon, the screen changes to a drawing tool which also has a sharing mode option to show mockups to others.

Low Fidelity Iterations

It was great to see the improvements through each iteration. Building upon my first iteration, I clarified the navigation icons and adjusted the gray tones. This helped me greatly prior to starting on the high fidelity iteration. It made me realize how important it is to flesh out the basic design before moving on to more detailed iterations.

Login Screen

I made the login more prominent and changed the new user sign up placement.

Welcome Screen

I added a notification banner at the top. Move the profile icon to the bottom next to the home button, This simplifies where the user can go. They have the two options of clicking the home button to return to the welcome menu or navigation menu bar.

Navigation Screen

I redesigned the tool bar. It also incorporates a log off icon instead of the previous prototype which had two separate locations of logging off. I think it looks cleaner and more polished.

Quick Draw Screen

I add a back arrow for the user to navigate back to the previous . I changed the icons to match their palette as well.

Test: Validation, Usability, Feedback

“(I need) Fast Iterations. (I) don’t want to spend time building. (I want) to see things instantly without a lot of setup.”–Participant 1

“Designing for changes depends on the hardware and platform. It forces me to use baked in tools or plugins that are all different and never the same standards.” –Participant 3

High Fidelity Wireframes

Through later useability testing, I was able to conduct improvements to Mokproto’s navigation for quicker efficiency.

High Fidelity Iterations

Some pain points users had difficulty with was locating the forgot password option and differentiating between the profile and setting icons. Thus, we want to increase task success rate.

Revision

  • Added Forgot Password Text to the Login Screen

  • Separated Log Off and User Profile

  • Added Log Off Confirmation

Accessibility

After an additional iteration of high fidelity design, I updated and improved accessibility functionality.

Color palette was adjusted for web contrast.

The end results concluded a stronger iteration of Mokproto beginning to root itself into actuality.

In Conclusion

The Mokproto project was a great experience. From conducting user tests to responding to user feedback as well as creating many design iterations, the list goes on and on! At some point, I would like to return to this project and revise it further. Mokproto has the potential of becoming a true mobile app in the future.

Creating the Art Exhibit

I went about designing my own art exhibit entirely from the ground up in Sketchup. I created the blueprint and layout of the Birdhouse gallery.

Sparrow Strom

Sparrow Strom is a photo essay on sparrows. I started documenting their lives in 2020. These feisty birds are one of the most common and urbanized so I felt the promotional materials should reﬂect an industrial feel.

In Conclusion

I look forward towards the future in developing my artwork further and the thought process that is involved in organizing an art exhibition. In the past, I have been in exhibits but not as a solo artist. This would be a nice goal to obtain!

Chardawn

The name Chardawn is a play on the french word , Chardon, which means thistle. Dawn is to incorporate the morning light.

For the brand, I wanted Chardawn to have a country feel. I envisioned using a thistle. I also wanted to incorporate the monarch into my logo. It was one of those days when you picture what you want to happen in regards to your project. Then when you go out to photograph, both thistle and monarch are there!

In Conclusion

I learned moodboards can be helpful and keep you on track with whatever feeling you want to incorporate with your design. Colors to match with nature help this brand convey its earthiness back to the prairie tone. I really enjoyed being able to first envision my idea and then to photograph it. The end result is pretty much how I imagined it.
